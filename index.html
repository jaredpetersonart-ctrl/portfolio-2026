Poster Process

I'd love to share some thoughts on my posters! First up, there's my Hypocycloid poster. I made this for the math department at Parkland College as part of a contest to display posters in the halls. I won! I wanted to blend art and science, so I decided to use parametric equations for a curve. I even put it on a blueprint to give it an architectural feel and included the formulas for the parametric equations.

Next, I designed a Garamond-style specimen poster. I picked the lowercase G because it's so beautiful, and I layered hundreds of Gs to form a large G in the center. This was to promote the Garamond typeface, and I won the contest again, having my design displayed in the graphic design department hallway.

The following two posters represent the illustration side. I created the Forest Frolic festival poster just for fun, to show I could make an illustrated forward poster for a local festival. The Detective Comics poster was made for one of my sons' birthdays. He and I are big fans of Batman and Robin.

Football Design Strategy

When I took on the Central Maroons football brand image project, I was asked to refresh the tertiary logo for the football team. I decided to strip away some of the old logo's elements and tidy things up a bit, not just to make it simpler, but to make it pop even more when it's smaller.

For the 2021 schedule, I wanted to create something that really gets the heart racing, using photography and type to grab attention. I quickly sketched out the layout I had in mind and used AI for the photographic assets. All the fonts were crafted in Adobe Illustrator using the Countach font, which is all about speed.

The third image is a flyer I put together to help promote freshman football. I included some fun facts and five reasons why someone should come out and play football.

During my final year at Central High School, I made some energetic videos for the stadium scoreboard. Even though I made several versions, I included these four to show off my work in Motion Graphics.
